About The Pilot

We are looking for forward-thinking schools to participate in a global digital learning study with University of Turku, Finland.

How it works?

1

Teacher training from Finland

Your school chooses 2-4 teachers to participate in the pilot. These teachers receive an online training course that teaches them all about how to use Eduten Playground with their students.

Don't worry, Eduten Playground is very easy to learn and use!

2

4 weekly lessons in school + homework

After the training the teachers replace one weekly mathematics lesson with working with Eduten Playground. The teachers will also assing homeowrk for the students through the system. You will be amazed how easy it is to check which students have done their homework.

3

Data analysis by University of Turku

After the 4 weeks our University team will analyze the data gathered from student activities. They will write a detailed analysis report based on their findings.

4

Pilot closing meeting

We will have a video call where go through the University report with the participating teachers and school management. The teachers will have the chance to summarize their and their students' experience using Eduten Playground.

Requirements

Your school must fulfil the following qualifications and conditions to participate in the global research study.

How it works?

1

Enthusiastic commitment

Your school must have at least 2 teachers that commit to using Eduten Playground once a week in their classroom + homework. We will provide training and support for the participating teachers, and the kids will love working with Eduten Playground.

2

Language and ICT skills

Your teachers and students will need to read and speak English, and have basic ICT skills to use the software.

3

Internet and computer access

Eduten Playground is accessed with a web browser. Each participating teacher and student needs to have a reasonably modern desktop computer, a laptop or a tablet with a reliable internet connection to use Eduten Playground.
